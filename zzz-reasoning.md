========Treatment Recommendation======== 

**Recommendation**: First-line treatment with osimertinib (80 mg daily) for advanced EGFR-mutant lung adenocarcinoma. Additional considerations:  
1. **Rationale**: The patient’s EGFR mutation (p.L747_T751delins) is a rare exon 19 deletion variant. While the NCCN guidelines explicitly prioritize osimertinib for common EGFR exon 19 deletions (e.g., E746_A750del) and exon 21 L858R mutations, osimertinib is also recommended for uncommon EGFR variants (e.g., S768I, L861Q, G719X) due to broader efficacy and CNS penetration. This aligns with NCCN’s inclusion of osimertinib as a preferred option for rare EGFR mutations (Reference: **10.6004/jnccn.2204.0023**). Afatinib is an alternative if osimertinib is unavailable, but osimertinib’s superior tolerability and activity in resistant settings support its use here.  
2. **Resistance monitoring**: Post-progression tumor biopsy or ctDNA analysis is critical to identify resistance mechanisms (e.g., T790M, C797S, MET amplification). The co-occurring TP53 and CDKN2A loss may accelerate resistance and worsen prognosis, necessitating frequent imaging (CT every 6–8 weeks) and ctDNA monitoring.  
3. **Subsequent therapy**: Upon progression, consider amivantamab-vmjw + chemotherapy (carboplatin/pemetrexed) if EGFR exon 20 insertion or MET amplification is detected (Reference: **10.6004/jnccn.2204.0023**). For T790M/C797S compound mutations (as in Case 2), clinical trials (e.g., BBT-176) or platinum-based chemotherapy + pemetrexed are options (Reference: **PMC10425724**).  
4. **Avoid immunotherapy**: PD-1/PD-L1 inhibitors are not recommended due to lack of efficacy in EGFR-mutant NSCLC and potential increased toxicity when combined with TKIs (Reference: **10.6004/jnccn.2204.0023**).  
5. **Supportive care**: Prophylactic management of osimertinib-related AEs (e.g., rash, diarrhea, interstitial lung disease) and palliative care for symptom control.

The **FLAURA2 trial** demonstrated superior progression-free survival (25.5 vs. 16.7 months) with osimertinib-chemotherapy versus osimertinib monotherapy in EGFR-mutant NSCLC (NCCN Reference: **10.6004/jnccn.2204.0023**).

---

**Key Monitoring & Resistance Management**:  
1. **Imaging & Biomarker Surveillance**:  
   - **CT scans every 6–8 weeks** to detect early progression.  
   - **ctDNA analysis at progression** to identify resistance mechanisms (e.g., T790M, C797S, MET amplification).  

2. **Screen for SCLC Transformation**:  
   - **Biopsy at progression**: Histologic transformation occurs in ~15% of EGFR-mutant NSCLC post-TKI. If confirmed, switch to **platinum-etoposide** (Reference: **PMC11369616**).  

3. **Resistance-Driven Subsequent Therapies**:  
   - **MET Amplification**: **Amivantamab-vmjw + chemotherapy** (NCCN category 1; Reference: **10.6004/jnccn.2204.0023**).  
   - **T790M/C797S**: Enroll in clinical trials for **BBT-176** (targets triple mutants; Reference: **PMC10425724**) or use platinum-pemetrexed.  
   - **Exon 20 Insertions**: Amivantamab-based regimens.  

---

**Contraindications**:  
- **Avoid PD-1/PD-L1 inhibitors**: No efficacy in EGFR-mutant NSCLC; increased risk of pneumonitis and other toxicities with TKIs (NCCN Reference: **10.6004/jnccn.2204.0023**).

- **Prophylaxis**: Manage osimertinib-related rash/diarrhea with topical steroids/antihistamines and loperamide.

- **Early Palliative Care**: Integrate for symptom control and quality-of-life optimization.
